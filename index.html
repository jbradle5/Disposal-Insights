EPA speeds up West Lake Landfill cleanup timeline after Zeldin visit

The EPA has accelerated the cleanup timeline for the radiologically contaminated West Lake Landfill in Missouri by over a year following a site visit from Administrator Lee Zeldin. Excavation work at the Superfund site is now projected to begin as early as September 2027 to address the hazardous waste.

East Tier News

Defending the North Country: The Battle for Forest Lake

New Hampshire residents and Governor Kelly Ayotte are fighting Casella Waste Systems' proposed landfill near Dalton's Forest Lake, arguing it threatens local conservation areas. Opponents are also battling state legislation that could strip local communities of their control over landfill expansions.

Community Pushback

Residents criticize Horry County landfill expansion plan

Horry County, South Carolina residents are fiercely opposing a proposed landfill expansion that would fill over 100 acres of wetlands to accommodate 23.4 million tons of waste. Opponents cite severe threats to water quality and endangered wildlife.

Talking trash: New Hanover County landfill explains ‘no out-of-county waste’ rule

The New Hanover County, NC landfill strictly enforces a rule rejecting any out-of-county waste to protect its operating permit and preserve its limited 30-year capacity. Staff conduct random load screenings and will double-charge and reject loads from individuals who lie about the origin.

The Environmental Defense of Scott County: The Roberta Landfill Denial

The Tennessee Department of Environment and Conservation officially denied a permit for the Roberta Landfill Phase II project due to significant concerns over water contamination and habitat destruction. Local leaders celebrated the decision as a major environmental victory.

The Juniper Ridge Expansion and the Public Benefit Mandate

The Maine Department of Environmental Protection determined that a 61-acre expansion of the Juniper Ridge Landfill serves a public benefit. However, the decision is being actively appealed by the Penobscot Nation and the Conservation Law Foundation over concerns of increased pollution.

The Rail-to-Landfill Initiative at Noble Environmental

Noble Environmental is shifting its plans to establish a waste-to-rail transfer system at its Greentree Landfill in PA, pivoting away from a truck transfer station due to community opposition. The Surface Transportation Board is currently reviewing the proposal.

Non-Landfill News (Incinerators, Processing, & Logistics)

Birmingham medical waste facility moves closer to approval

A proposed non-hazardous medical waste facility using ozone gas technology in Birmingham's Kingston community has received strong public support for its potential to create jobs and tax revenue. The city council is expected to vote soon on the project, which would prevent local medical waste from being shipped out of state.

Philly trash incineration: city could extend contract

Philadelphia is considering a one-year extension of its waste disposal contracts, including a controversial agreement with Reworld's Chester trash incinerator, to further evaluate environmental impacts. Environmental advocates and some city council members are pushing to ban waste incineration entirely due to severe air pollution concerns in marginalized communities.

EREF: 'profound' regulatory changes shaped 30 years of medical waste incineration

Strict federal emissions standards enacted since the 1990s have forced the closure of 99% of U.S. medical waste incinerators, leaving only a few highly regulated facilities operating today. To meet growing demand for medical waste destruction in the West, modern incineration facilities like Stericycle's recent $110 million plant in Nevada have been developed to exceed environmental standards.
