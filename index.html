West Tier News

EPA Fines California Landfills; Orange County Faces Disposal Crunch

The EPA fined multiple California municipal solid waste operations for Clean Air Act violations, including massive methane exceedances at landfills. Simultaneously, local capacity is shrinking rapidly as regional infrastructure reaches max density, worsening a localized disposal crunch.

EPA Enforcement

Orange County Landfill Fees to Spike 53%

Beginning July 2026, Orange County, California will increase its landfill tipping fees from $43.89 to $67 per ton under new 10-year WISE Agreements. This overnight 53% hike aims to fund rising capacity construction, regulatory compliance, and long-term maintenance costs.

East Tier News

Radioactive Waste Shipments to Wayne County Landfill Permanently Halted

A Michigan circuit judge permanently blocked the Wayne Disposal landfill from accepting low-level radioactive materials from historic atomic weapons sites. The court cited elevated cancer and health risks to highly populated surrounding communities and nearby schools.

Hazardous Waste

Apollo Global Management Acquires Majority Interest in Noble Environmental

New York-based asset management firm Apollo Global Management has purchased a majority stake in Noble Environmental, a Pennsylvania-based regional waste provider. Noble manages solid waste infrastructure and captures landfill gas for its growing renewable natural gas business.

Acquisition

Horry County Shifts Landfill Expansion Funds to Alternative Waste Studies

Horry County leaders approved a budget amendment redirecting $300,000 from landfill expansion plans toward studying a construction and demolition transfer station. This decision avoids an expansion project that would have negatively impacted over 100 acres of local wetlands.

Diversion

Hunterdon County Transfer Station Sale Nears Completion

The sale of the state-owned Petticoat Lane transfer station in New Jersey to Waste Management Inc. for $3.4 million is nearly finalized. The transaction was delayed for two years due to a facility fire in 2025 and required state legislation to declare the property surplus Landfill.

Divestiture

Two Areas of Ontario County Landfill to be Capped This Year

Ontario County landfill officials plan to install permanent capping over two major sections of the facility later this year to mitigate ongoing odor and environmental concerns. This localized containment strategy follows a history of regional trash capacity battles and strict environmental quality mandates.

Capping

Northeast Solid Waste Disposal Capacity Shrinks as Exporting Increases

A regional report highlights that limited local landfill capacity has caused municipal solid waste generated in the Northeast to be heavily exported out of state via rail. Disposals inside generating states fell significantly as regional trash infrastructure reaches max capacity.

Non-Landfill News (Incinerators, Processing, & Logistics)

Union Pacific Refutes Red-State Attorneys General Claims Over Merger

Union Pacific defended its revised merger application with Norfolk Southern against claims from six state attorneys general who argued the filing lacked required information. In an STB filing, the railroad maintained it addressed all regulatory requirements and that the merger will boost U.S. supply chain competitiveness.

Railroad Merger

Miami-Dade Incinerator Project Cast in Doubt Over Rate Freeze

Miami-Dade County Mayor Daniella Levine Cava suggested abandoning plans for a new $2 billion waste-to-energy plant after commissioners rejected a residential garbage rate increase. Freezing these rates forces $4.6 million in upcoming solid waste budget cuts.
